Cross Section Tool

As an individual research and development project conducted under my employment at Babcock, I produced a reusable system that allows cross sectioning to be simulated on models within Unity to create engaging and effective training media.

The tool is focused around using planes that defined where the cross section would occur and on which Materials. It also allows:

  • Cross sections to be moved and rotated even during runtime
  • Multiple planes to be involve with cross sectioning a Material, useful for making all kinds of slicing effects
  • Clipping and Volumetric modes (explained below)

This illustration demonstrates the different cross section types applied to the enclosed green cube:

The cross section involves the back faces of the effected Material being exposed and the illusion that the cross sectioned surface is flat comes from only allowing a bold colour to be presented on the back faces.

Clipping describes when the cross sectioning has just removed the effected part of the target Material giving a floating appearance, which is useful for the most external object involved in the cross section.

Volumetric clips the target Material and hides its front faces which allows the surrounding object to seem that its volume is filled and the enclosed object is solidified within it.
